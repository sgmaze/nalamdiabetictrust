Bridging the Healthcare Gap in Coimbatore

[cite_start]

Nalam Diabetic Prevention Trust is a student-led non-profit initiative dedicated to combating the diabetes epidemic in Tamil Nadu, India[cite: 53]. [cite_start]Founded by five high school students from the USA with deep family roots in Coimbatore, the trust seeks to bridge the gap in healthcare through early detection, prevention, and community awareness[cite: 54].

Our Mission [cite_start]"Our mission is to shift the local health paradigm from late-stage disease management to early-stage prevention, ensuring that the people of Coimbatore and surrounding villages have the tools to live healthier, longer lives." [cite: 55]

The Problem

Late Detection

[cite_start]

Many residents in the Coimbatore region only discover they are diabetic after complications arise[cite: 57].

Lack of Prioritization

[cite_start]

Regular preventative testing (like A1C) is not a common practice due to costs and lack of awareness[cite: 58].

Cultural Barriers

[cite_start]

Health information is often not available in accessible Tamil-language formats that resonate with local dietary and lifestyle habits[cite: 59].

Our Solution

Free A1C Testing

[cite_start]

Partnering with a vetted local Coimbatore laboratory to provide gold-standard testing for blood sugar levels[cite: 68].

Targeted Education

[cite_start]

Providing immediate, bilingual (Tamil/English) guidance on nutrition, local foods to eat/avoid, and exercise routines[cite: 69].

Data-Driven Follow-up

[cite_start]

Using a WhatsApp-based system to track high-risk individuals and remind them to return for a 3 or 6 month re-test depending on their risk level[cite: 70].
